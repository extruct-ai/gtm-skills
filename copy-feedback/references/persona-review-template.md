# Persona Review Template

Full templates for world simulation, professional reality, and skeptical buyer roast.

## World Simulation Template

```markdown
# World Simulation: [Full Name]
**Role:** [Title] at [Company]
**Date researched:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Sources:** [LinkedIn, company site, press, Perplexity queries]

## Raw Research
- **Career arc:** [Full career history — where they started, key moves, how they got here]
- **Education:** [If found]
- **LinkedIn About:** [Verbatim or paraphrased]
- **Public statements:** [Direct quotes with sources]
- **Company news:** [Recent events, launches, strategy shifts]

## Daily Reality
- **What their day looks like:** [Meeting-heavy? Heads-down? Travel? Managing a team of N?]
- **Tools they live in:** [CRM, Slack, email, dashboards, spreadsheets]
- **What keeps them up at night:** [The 1-2 unsolved problems]
- **Who they report to:** [Title of their boss, what that boss cares about]
- **Who reports to them:** [Team size, functions]

## Psychology and Decision-Making
- **Decision style:** [Data-driven / gut / consensus / authority]
- **Communication preference:** [Terse / detailed / formal / casual / story-driven]
- **Risk appetite:** [Early adopter / wait-for-proof / market-leader-only]
- **Trust signals:** [Referrals / data / case studies / free trial / brand recognition]
- **Pet peeves:** [What annoys this type of person — jargon, flattery, vagueness]

## Inbox Behavior
- **Email volume estimate:** [50/day for IC, 100-200/day for VP+]
- **Cold email tolerance:** [Opens most? Scans subject only? Assistant filters?]
- **Reply blockers:** [Too busy, doesn't trust unknowns, needs social proof first]
- **Reply triggers:** [Specific, relevant, low-effort CTA, credible sender]
- **Preferred response style:** [Quick one-liner? Forwards to team? Asks for more info?]
```

## Red Flag Checklist

Always check for these. Each one reduces reply probability.

**Spam signals:**
- Generic opener that could apply to anyone in their role
- "I noticed" + something obvious from the company homepage
- Flattery disguised as personalization ("impressive growth")
- Subject line with their first name and nothing else

**Pain mismatch:**
- Opener references a problem this person doesn't have (based on research)
- Pain point is real but not THEIR pain — belongs to a different role
- Pain is too high-level (company pain) when they care about team-level pain
- Pain is hypothetical — "companies like yours often face..."

**Bridge failures:**
- Solution description is too abstract — no concrete connection to their workflow
- Example query or use case doesn't match their vertical/function
- Leap from "here's the pain" to "here's our tool" is too fast
- No example of how the tool actually solves the specific pain mentioned

**CTA problems:**
- Asking for a meeting on a first cold email to a VP+
- CTA is generic ("happy to chat?") instead of specific to their work
- CTA requires them to do work ("check out our docs")
- CTA has no clear value exchange — what do they get for responding?

**Credibility gaps:**
- Proof point is from a different industry than the prospect's
- Case study company is too different in size/stage
- Metric cited is impressive but irrelevant to their KPIs
- No proof point at all

**Tone mismatches:**
- Too casual for a C-suite / VP executive
- Too formal for an ops manager or practitioner
- Uses jargon the prospect wouldn't use
- Email reads like a marketer wrote it, prospect is a builder/operator
